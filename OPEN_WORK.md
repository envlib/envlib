- [x] ~~**[s3func] Fix bakery-lock weaknesses**~~ — **RESOLVED 2026-07-03** (see the 0.9.0 review+release item above: root-caused via instrumented campaign, fixed, validated 0-violations). Original record:
  1. *Accumulation bug* (by inspection, `s3func/locking.py:175-185`): `locked` is reassigned per iteration in `_check_for_older_objs`, so only the last-listed contender counts — with 3+ simultaneous writers, two can both acquire. One-line fix (`locked = locked or ...` or early-return).
  2. *LIST-after-PUT visibility race* (observed live): `acquire()` PUTs its lock objects then immediately LISTs for competitors; a competitor whose lock PUT hasn't propagated into LIST results yet is invisible, so two writers can both acquire even with only 2 contenders. Mitigation shape: self-visibility check (poll LIST until your own lock objects appear, then evaluate competitors) + settle-delay re-list; reproducer: looped two-process acquire with mutual-exclusion assertion under parallel load. Corroboration: Mike's own s3func lock tests historically showed rare, irreproducible random failures — consistent with this window. Until fixed, truly-simultaneous first-contact writers to the same remote remain a small race window; ebooklet 0.8.4's post-lock metadata refresh protects every case where the lock actually serializes.
